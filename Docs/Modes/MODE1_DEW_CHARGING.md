# Mode 1 — DEW Charging

## Overview

Mode 1 routes harvested recoil energy directly to capacitor banks that feed a co-located or networked directed energy weapon. The conventional gun's recoil literally charges the laser.

## How It Works

1. Gun fires. Barrel recoils through linear electromagnetic generator (LEG).
2. LEG converts kinetic energy to AC electrical current via electromagnetic induction.
3. Power Management Unit (PMU) rectifies AC output and routes to Capacitor Bank Array (CBA).
4. CBA stores energy at high charge/discharge rates (supercapacitors).
5. When sufficient energy is accumulated, fire control authorizes DEW engagement.
6. CBA discharges into the directed energy weapon (e.g., HELIOS laser).

## Energy Per Shot (50% Conversion Efficiency)

- Raw recoil energy: ~125 kJ
- Post-conversion electrical: 62.5 kJ
- Post-capacitor storage (90% round-trip): **56.25 kJ usable per shot**

## What This Powers

The Navy's HELIOS laser operates at 60 kW. A 1-second engagement requires 60 kJ.

- **1 gun round ≈ 1 laser shot** (at conservative efficiency)
- At 20 rounds/min: enough energy for ~18 one-second laser engagements per minute
- In a 5-minute gun engagement (100 rounds): ~93 laser shots enabled

## Tactical Application

During a drone swarm engagement where the Mk 45 is already firing at inbound targets, Mode 1 simultaneously harvests recoil energy and feeds it to the laser. The gun kills drones at range; the laser kills drones up close. Both weapons operate in tandem, with the gun powering the laser as a byproduct of its own operation.

## Key Advantage

Mode 1 creates an energetic coupling between the conventional gun and the DEW that does not currently exist in any naval weapons architecture. Firing the gun charges the laser. The two weapons become a unified system rather than competing for ship resources.
