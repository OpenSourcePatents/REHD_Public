# Mode 2 — Rate-of-Fire Boost

## Overview

Mode 2 stores harvested recoil energy mechanically or electromagnetically and uses it to accelerate the barrel's return-to-battery stroke. This directly reduces cycle time between shots, increasing rate of fire without modifying ammunition, propellant, or feed systems.

## How It Works

1. Gun fires. Barrel recoils through the REHD mechanism.
2. Instead of (or in addition to) electrical conversion, energy is stored in a Mechanical Energy Return System (MERS).
3. MERS options include: gas spring compression, flywheel spin-up, or electromagnetic linear motor charging.
4. On the return stroke, MERS releases stored energy to drive the barrel forward faster than the conventional recoil spring alone.
5. Faster return = shorter cycle time = higher rate of fire.

## The Positive Feedback Loop

This mode creates a unique dynamic:

- Faster firing → more recoil events per minute → more energy harvested per minute
- More energy harvested → faster return strokes → even faster firing
- This continues up to the structural and feed-system limits of the gun mount

The Mk 45's current max ROF of ~20 rounds/min is limited partly by the recoil/return cycle time. Mode 2 could potentially push this toward the feed system's mechanical limit rather than the recoil system's limit.

## Tactical Application

In a surface engagement requiring maximum gun output — shore bombardment, anti-surface warfare, or sustained anti-drone fire — Mode 2 maximizes rounds on target per unit time. The gun becomes its own force multiplier.

## Key Advantage

Mode 2 is the most mechanically straightforward REHD mode. It keeps energy in the mechanical domain (no electrical conversion losses) and directly enhances the gun's primary function: putting rounds on target faster. It requires no external systems — the improvement is entirely within the gun mount itself.

## Inspiration

The spring energy storage concept in Mode 2 was inspired by the tri-stage adaptive suspension system in the [FCK-U System](https://github.com/OpenSourcePatents/FCK-U-System), which uses staged spring compression to store and return kinetic energy in a vehicle safety architecture. The same principle of "don't waste kinetic energy — store it and give it back" applies here.
