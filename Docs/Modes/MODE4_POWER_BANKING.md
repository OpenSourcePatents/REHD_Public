# Mode 4 — Supplemental Ship Power Banking

## Overview

Mode 4 routes harvested recoil energy to a dedicated Supplemental Power Bank (SPB) for later use. When the ship is not in an immediate power deficit — when DEWs aren't actively firing, when ROF boost isn't needed — the energy goes into savings. Think of it as a combat energy savings account.

## How It Works

1. Gun fires. REHD harvests recoil energy as in other modes.
2. PMU routes electrical energy to the Supplemental Power Bank (SPB).
3. SPB stores energy in longer-duration storage: larger capacitor banks, battery systems, or flywheel energy storage.
4. Stored energy is available to the ship's electrical grid on demand, independent of gun firing status.
5. Ship can withdraw banked energy minutes or hours later for any purpose: laser engagement, radar power, damage control systems, propulsion assist.

## Energy Accumulation

At 50% conversion efficiency with a single Mk 45 at max ROF:

| Duration | Rounds | Energy Banked |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| 1 minute | 20 | 1.125 MJ |
| 5 minutes | 100 | 5.625 MJ |
| 10 minutes | 200 | 11.25 MJ |
| 30 minutes (sustained fire) | 600 | 33.75 MJ |

A Ticonderoga with two guns doubles these figures.

## Tactical Applications

**Combat banking:** During a sustained engagement, the ship simultaneously fights and charges. Energy accumulates as a byproduct of combat operations.

**Training dividend:** Every routine gunnery exercise becomes a power generation event. Fire 100 training rounds during a practice shoot, bank 5.625 MJ. The ship gets better at shooting AND gets a power reserve. Training literally pays for itself in energy.

**Shore bombardment surplus:** During NSFS missions firing hundreds of rounds over hours, Mode 4 builds a massive energy reserve. A 500-round bombardment mission banks ~28 MJ — enough to meaningfully supplement ship power for subsequent operations.

**Pre-engagement charging:** Before entering a threat area, the ship fires a deliberate gunnery exercise in Mode 4 to pre-charge the SPB. Enter the fight with a full power reserve.

## Key Advantage

Mode 4 decouples energy generation from energy use in time. The gun fires now; the energy is used later. This is the mode that most directly addresses Admiral Boxall's power deficit concern, because it creates a supplemental power source that exists entirely outside the ship's normal generation capacity. It's found energy — energy that was being thrown away as waste heat — now available for any purpose the ship needs.

## The "Combat Savings Account" Metaphor

Every round fired is a deposit. Every laser shot, radar pulse, or system activation is a withdrawal. The ship's fire control system manages the balance. In a sustained deployment with daily gunnery, the SPB accumulates energy continuously. The ship gets richer in power with every engagement.
