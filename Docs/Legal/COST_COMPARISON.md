# Cost Comparison — REHD vs. Alternative Power Solutions

## The Problem

Shipboard directed energy weapons require electrical power that existing ships cannot generate. Every proposed solution has cost and timeline implications.

## Comparison

| Solution | Est. Cost/Ship | Timeline | Hull Modifications | Fuel Impact | Notes |
|----------|---------------|----------|-------------------|-------------|-------|
| **REHD retrofit** | **<$2M/gun** | **12–18 months** | **None** | **None** | Retrofit during scheduled maintenance |
| Additional gas turbine | $50–100M | 3–5 years | Major hull cuts | +fuel consumption | Competes for hull volume |
| Nuclear propulsion | $1B+ | 10+ years | Complete redesign | Eliminated | Only viable for new-build |
| Dedicated DEW generator | $20–50M | 2–4 years | Moderate | +fuel consumption | Competes for space/weight |
| Battery storage system | $5–15M | 1–2 years | Moderate | None | Stores power but doesn't generate it |
| Flywheel storage | $3–10M | 1–2 years | Moderate | None | Stores power but doesn't generate it |

## Cost Context

### REHD vs. Missile Costs

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|

A single REHD installation costs less than one SM-2 missile. Every drone killed by a REHD-powered laser instead of an SM-2 saves $2.4M and preserves a VLS cell.

### The Red Sea Math

The Navy has spent nearly **$1 billion** in interceptor missiles during Red Sea operations since October 2023.

Equipping every Mk 45 in the entire U.S. Navy with REHD would cost roughly **$160M** — less than one-sixth of what has been spent on missiles in a single theater.

## Key Differentiators

**REHD is the only solution that:**
- Generates power without consuming fuel
- Requires no hull modifications
- Doesn't compete for space with other systems
- Produces power precisely when combat demand is highest
- Uses infrastructure that already exists (the gun mount)
- Converts waste energy that is already being produced

Every other solution adds cost, complexity, and demands on the ship's design. REHD subtracts waste.
