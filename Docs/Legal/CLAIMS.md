# Provisional Claims Summary

## Claims 1–7 (Public Release)

**Claim 1:** A weapons-energy system comprising a linear electromagnetic generator integrated into the recoil mechanism of a conventional firearm or artillery piece, wherein the kinetic energy of the recoil stroke is converted to electrical energy.

**Claim 2:** The system of Claim 1, further comprising a power management unit that dynamically routes harvested electrical energy to one or more of: a directed energy weapon power supply (Mode 1), a mechanical rate-of-fire acceleration system (Mode 2), an electromagnetic projectile boost system (Mode 3), or a supplemental power storage bank (Mode 4).

**Claim 3:** The system of Claim 2, wherein the power management unit receives mode selection commands from a fire control system and can blend energy distribution across multiple modes simultaneously.

**Claim 4:** The system of Claim 1, wherein the linear electromagnetic generator is integrated into a naval gun mount, and the harvested energy is used to charge capacitor banks that supply power to a shipboard directed energy weapon.

**Claim 5:** A method of operating a weapons-energy system comprising: firing a conventional gun; harvesting recoil kinetic energy via electromagnetic induction; and routing harvested energy based on real-time tactical requirements to one or more of four operating modes.

**Claim 6:** The system of Claim 2, further comprising a supplemental power bank that accumulates energy during gun engagements and makes stored energy available to the host platform's electrical grid on demand, independent of gun firing status.

**Claim 7:** The system of Claim 1, applied to a naval surface combatant, wherein multiple gun mounts each incorporate REHD systems and aggregate their harvested energy output into a shared power bus.
