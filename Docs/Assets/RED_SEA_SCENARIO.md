# Red Sea Engagement Scenario — REHD in Action

## Context

Since October 2023, U.S. Navy destroyers in the Red Sea have engaged in the most sustained naval combat since World War II. Houthi forces have launched hundreds of drones, cruise missiles, and ballistic missiles at commercial shipping and U.S. warships. Navy destroyers have used SM-2 missiles ($2.4M each), ESSM Sea Sparrows ($1.7M each), and increasingly their Mk 45 5-inch guns to counter these threats.

The cost exchange ratio is devastating: million-dollar missiles against thousand-dollar drones. The Navy has spent nearly $1 billion in interceptor missiles in Red Sea operations.

---

## Scenario: 20-Target Houthi Swarm Attack

### Without REHD (Current Reality)

**T+0:00** — SPY-6 detects inbound swarm: 18 one-way attack drones, 2 anti-ship cruise missiles.

**T+0:30** — SM-2 missiles engage cruise missiles at range.
- 2× SM-2 expended: **$4.8M**

**T+1:00** — ESSM engages medium-range drone targets.
- 6× ESSM expended: **$10.2M**

**T+2:00** — Mk 45 opens fire on close-range drones. Phalanx CIWS engages leakers.
- 60 rounds of 5-inch: **$48K**

**T+4:00** — Engagement complete.
- **Total cost: ~$15M+ in interceptors**
- **8 VLS cells emptied** (cannot reload at sea)
- HELIOS laser available but power-constrained — limited contribution
- Ship enters next engagement with depleted missile inventory

### With REHD Installed

**T+0:00** — SPY-6 detects same inbound swarm.

**T+0:30** — SM-2 engages cruise missiles at range.
- 2× SM-2 expended: $4.8M (unchanged — REHD doesn't help with long-range intercepts)

**T+1:00** — Mk 45 opens fire on drones at 13 NM. **REHD activates Mode 1 (DEW Charging).**
- Each round: 56.25 kJ deposited into laser capacitor bank

**T+1:15** — After 5 rounds, 281 kJ banked. Not enough for full HELIOS shot yet. Gun continues firing.

**T+1:30** — 10 rounds fired. 562 kJ banked. Fire control authorizes HELIOS.
- **HELIOS fires 1-second burst (60 kJ) at inbound drone. Kill.**
- 502 kJ remaining in bank. Gun continues firing and recharging.

**T+1:45** — 15 more rounds fired. Bank at ~1,346 kJ.
- **HELIOS fires again. Second drone kill.**
- This drone would have required an ESSM ($1.7M). Killed by laser for pennies.

**T+2:00** — Gun and laser working in tandem. Every ~10 rounds generates a laser shot.
- ESSM expenditure reduced from 6 to 2 (laser handled 4 close-range targets)
- **ESSM savings: 4 × $1.7M = $6.8M saved**

**T+3:00** — Swarm thinning. Fire control switches to **Mode 4 (Banking).**
- Remaining gun rounds deposit energy into supplemental reserve.

**T+4:00** — Engagement complete. 80 rounds fired.
- **REHD harvested: ~4.5 MJ total**
- **~6 HELIOS shots enabled from gun recoil alone**
- **4 fewer ESSM missiles expended**
- **4 VLS cells preserved** for next engagement
- **$6.8M in missile costs avoided**
- SPB holds ~2 MJ reserve for next engagement

### Net Impact

| Metric | Without REHD | With REHD | Delta |
|--------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| SM-2 expended | 2 | 2 | — |
| ESSM expended | 6 | 2 | -4 missiles |
| VLS cells consumed | 8 | 4 | -4 cells |
| Missile cost | ~$15M | ~$8.2M | **-$6.8M** |
| Laser shots enabled | 0 | 6 | +6 |
| Post-engagement power reserve | 0 | 2 MJ | +2 MJ |

**Over a 6-month Red Sea deployment with dozens of engagements, REHD could save hundreds of millions of dollars in interceptor missiles and preserve hundreds of VLS cells.**
