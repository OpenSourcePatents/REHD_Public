# REHD — Executive Brief (60-Second Summary)

**FOR:** Naval leadership concerned with shipboard power generation capacity for directed energy weapons

---

**THE PROBLEM:** The Navy cannot generate enough electrical power on existing destroyers to operate directed energy weapons. Flight III Burkes are maxed powering SPY-6. Every proposed solution — new generators, nuclear propulsion, ship redesign — costs billions and takes years.

**THE SOLUTION:** Every time a naval gun fires, it generates ~125,000 joules of recoil energy currently dumped as waste heat. REHD captures that energy via linear electromagnetic generators in the recoil mechanism and converts it to usable electrical power.

**THE MATH (Conservative — 50% Efficiency):**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Usable energy per shot | 56.25 kJ |
| Continuous power at max ROF | 18.75 kW |
| Energy in 10-min engagement (200 rds) | 11.25 MJ |
| Carrier strike group (6 mounts) | 112.5 kW continuous |
| HELIOS shots per 100 gun rounds | ~93 |

**THE COST:** <$2M per gun mount retrofit. No new generators. No hull mods. No additional fuel. Less than one SM-2 missile.

**THE APPLICABILITY:** Any vessel mounting a Mk 45 or equivalent recoil-operated gun. ~70 Burke DDGs, ~9 Ticonderoga CGs, future Trump-class, plus all allied Mk 45 navies worldwide.

**THE BOTTOM LINE:** The Navy is spending $2.4M per SM-2 to shoot down $2,000 drones while throwing away free energy from every gun round fired. REHD turns the gun into a generator.
