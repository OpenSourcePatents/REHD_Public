# Message for Rear Admiral Boxall

**RE: "Out of Schlitz with regard to power"**

---

Admiral Boxall,

You identified a problem: the Navy's surface combatants cannot generate enough electrical power to operate the directed energy weapons they need. You said it plainly — the fleet is out of room on the power budget, and every new system competes for watts that don't exist.

I heard that problem, and I think I have part of the answer.

Every time a Mark 45 fires, approximately 125,000 joules of kinetic energy slams into the recoil buffers and becomes waste heat. At 20 rounds per minute, that's over a megajoule per minute of energy being generated and thrown away. Across a carrier strike group, across a sustained engagement, across thousands of rounds — that's an enormous amount of energy that already exists in the system and costs nothing to produce.

REHD (Recoil Energy Harvesting and Directed Energy Integration) captures that energy with linear electromagnetic generators integrated into the recoil mechanism, and routes it to where the ship needs it: laser capacitor banks, rate-of-fire acceleration, or a supplemental power reserve.

The math works. At a conservative 50% conversion efficiency — well below what modern linear generators actually achieve — a single Mk 45 at max rate produces 18.75 kW of continuous equivalent power. A Ticonderoga with two guns doubles that. A strike group aggregates it further. It won't replace a gas turbine generator, but it doesn't need to. It supplements the power budget during combat — precisely when DEW demand is highest — using energy the ship is already producing.

The cost is less than $2M per gun mount. That's less than one SM-2 missile. It requires no new generators, no hull modifications, no removal of existing systems. It's a retrofit to existing recoil mechanisms, installable during scheduled maintenance.

The full technical disclosure, energy budget calculations, a Red Sea engagement scenario walkthrough, fleet-wide applicability analysis, and cost comparison are all in this repository. Everything here is open source — no royalty, no charge, no strings.

One portion of the concept (Mode 3 — Kinetic Energy Assist) has been redacted from the public version pending ITAR review, as it touches on electromagnetic projectile acceleration technology. The unrestricted version is available to verified DoD personnel upon request.
